Tips & Tricks

• Prep Ingredients Before Cooking (Mise en Place): Always measure, chop, and prepare all your ingredients before you start cooking. It keeps the process smooth and ensures you do not forget anything.
• Taste as You Go: Regularly taste your dish while cooking to adjust seasonings and ensure the flavors are balanced.
• Master the Basics: Learn essential techniques like boiling, sautéing, roasting, and baking. Once you master the basics, you can experiment and create your own recipes.
• Do not Overcrowd the Pan: When frying or roasting, leave enough space between ingredients. Overcrowding lowers the temperature, resulting in steaming instead of browning.
• Use Fresh Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs and quality spices can elevate the flavor of any dish. Remember to add fresh herbs at the end for a burst of flavor.
• Let Meat Rest Before Cutting: After cooking, allow meat to rest for a few minutes. This helps retain its juices, making it more tender and flavorful.
• Save Pasta Water: When cooking pasta, save a cup of the starchy water. It is perfect for making silky sauces that cling to the pasta.
• Invest in Good Kitchen Tools: A sharp knife, sturdy cutting board, and quality pans can make cooking easier and more enjoyable.
• Experiment and Have Fun!
